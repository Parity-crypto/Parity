The Reverse-Difficulty Loop

Traditional mining scales rewards to the strongest machines. Parity flips the structural layout completely: the more powerful your workstation, the more difficult the work threshold becomes. This guarantees low-tier wallets consistently generate steady asset flows.

Zero Hardware Strain

Because the network does not rely on computational saturation or continuous brute-force guessing patterns, our client consumes negligible resources. Your system runs quietly without degradation, thermal limits, or hardware fatigue.

Ultra-Low Power Access

Parity is fully scalable. The algorithm is light enough to run natively on tiny, hyper-efficient hardware configurations—including a Raspberry Pi 2. Decentralized allocation belongs to real human endpoints, not data centers.

Knowledge Base

Frequently Asked Questions.

Is Parity intended to be a stablecoin?
Yes, Parity is designed from the ground up to establish a stable, predictable growth trajectory. By restricting massive mining farms through the reverse-difficulty loop, the coin prevents sudden supply dumps and aggressive market volatility caused by institutional whales.
Will this harm my personal computer?
No. The Parity algorithm bypasses traditional hashing brute-force mechanics. It runs gracefully in the background with near-zero CPU and GPU footprint, meaning your fans won't spin up, temperatures won't rise, and component lifespan remains unaffected.
Why is the mining client closed-source?
To protect the "Proof of Equality" mechanics. If the core validation algorithm were open-source, malicious actors could compile modified versions to bypass the reverse-difficulty restrictions, essentially creating artificial bottlenecks and ruining the fair distribution model.
Can I run Parity on multiple devices?
You can, however, the network limits excessive concentration from identical IP subnets to maintain decentralization. We encourage users to run single nodes on their daily driver laptops, desktops, or dedicated ultra-low-power devices like a Raspberry Pi.
